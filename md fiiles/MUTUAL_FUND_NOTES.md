# 🏦 Mutual Fund

## 📝 What is a Mutual Fund?

Imagine you and your 9 friends decide to go on a trip.

Everyone contributes ₹1000.

Total money collected:

₹1000 × 10 = ₹10,000

Instead of each person managing the money separately, you appoint one responsible friend to manage the entire ₹10,000.

This friend decides:

- Where to stay.
- What to eat.
- How to spend the money wisely.

A Mutual Fund works in a similar way.

Many investors pool their money together, and a professional called a Fund Manager manages this money on their behalf.

---

## 🎯 Simple Definition

A Mutual Fund is a pool of money collected from many investors that is managed by professional experts and invested in different assets such as stocks and bonds.

---

## 👨‍💼 Real-Life Example

Suppose:

You invest: ₹10,000

Thousands of other investors also invest money.

Total money collected by the fund becomes: ₹100 Crore.

A professional Fund Manager then invests this money in companies like:

- Reliance
- TCS
- Infosys
- HDFC Bank
- ICICI Bank

Instead of investing all the money in a single company.

---

## 🤔 Why Do People Invest in Mutual Funds?

### 1. Professional Management

Experts manage your money.

### 2. Diversification

Your money gets invested in many companies, reducing risk.

### 3. Small Investment

You can start investing with a small amount.

### 4. Convenience

You don't have to research every stock yourself.

---

## 🧑 Key People Involved

### Investor

A person who invests money.

### Fund Manager

A professional who decides where to invest the pooled money.

### AMC (Asset Management Company)

The company that manages the Mutual Fund.

Examples:

- HDFC Mutual Fund
- SBI Mutual Fund
- ICICI Prudential Mutual Fund

---

## 📌 One-Line Summary

Mutual Fund = Many people + Pooled money + Expert management.

# 💰 NAV (Net Asset Value)

## 📝 What is NAV?

NAV stands for **Net Asset Value**.

In simple words, NAV is the **price of one unit of a Mutual Fund**.

You can think of NAV as the price tag of a mutual fund.

---

## 👨‍🏫 Real-Life Example

Imagine you and your friends contribute money to buy a pizza.

Total money collected = ₹1000

Suppose the pizza is divided into 10 equal slices.

Price of one slice:

₹1000 / 10 = ₹100

So, the price of one slice is ₹100.

Similarly, in a Mutual Fund:

- Total money invested by everyone is divided into many units.
- The price of one unit is called **NAV**.

---

## 🎯 Example

Suppose:

Current NAV = ₹50

You invest = ₹10,000

Number of units you get:

10000 / 50 = 200 units

So, you now own **200 units** of the mutual fund.

---

## 📈 What Happens if NAV Increases?

Suppose after some time:

New NAV = ₹60

Value of your investment:

200 × 60 = ₹12,000

Profit:

₹12,000 - ₹10,000 = ₹2,000

---

## 🤔 Why Does NAV Change?

NAV changes every day because the prices of the stocks and bonds held by the mutual fund keep changing.

If the investments perform well → NAV increases 📈

If the investments perform poorly → NAV decreases 📉

---

## 📌 One-Line Summary

NAV is the price of one unit of a Mutual Fund.

# 🏷️ AMFI Scheme Code

AMFI Scheme Code is a unique numerical identifier assigned to every mutual fund scheme in India.

It helps in uniquely identifying a mutual fund scheme across different platforms and databases.

Example:

125497 → HDFC Top 100 Direct Plan Growth
119551 → SBI Bluechip Fund

In this project, AMFI codes are used to fetch NAV data from the MFAPI and to validate records across datasets.
